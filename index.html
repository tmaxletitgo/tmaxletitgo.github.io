Father To My Blessings

Nadina works as a reporter for a private television station in her home country. She writes a series of e-mails to her father, who died during a war that tore apart her beloved nation. She relates her story of resilience, perseverance, and courage after a breakthrough in communication allows people from this world to communicate with people in the world of the living dead. What she shares is her struggle to take care of her siblings, who are still in school when their father passes. Nadina works her socks off to feed these children, pay for their school fees, and see them become independent. Even though Nadina scriticizes the political, economic, and social structures of her country—an allegory for the African states—and calls for an end to rampant poverty, wars, and conflicts, she is still appointed Minister of Information. With this new status, her dream to fight poverty and bring about peace has just begun.
By Olivier SEMPIGA
